7 Top Tips To Score The Highest Marks In Your Exams

Always feel positive

Positive attitude is the key to solve many of the problems which you face in your life. During exam time, this is an important feature to have in you for success and crack your exams with flying colors. Attitude is important for all students because it reflects your personality as well as your confidence or self-confidence. It always takes you on top of everything, whether it is for exams or interviews or your life itself. Positive attitude will take you on through the door of success and make you feel full of self-confidence. So, never think anything negative in your mind and be fulfilled with thoughts of positivity!

Plan well for studying

Never try to read anything or to study or cram just before the exam time, even if your friend asks you to prepare for some topic he has missed or left during preparation for the exams. Close your book an hour before the exam starts and stay relaxed, unworried and full of self-confidence. Also get up early in the morning and take another review of the important topics and make yourself filled with confidence, as confidence is the main property to score well. The night before the exam you should go to bed early to make your brain as well as body relax a bit and to be well prepared for the exams, as our brain too needs a rest to be fresh and fine for the exam.

During exam time

Check out all the things you require during exam time like pen, pencil, sketch pens, rubber, ruler, sharpener etc. Each and everything, whether it is small or big matters a lot during your exam time. Read all the instructions carefully before starting the paper and keep them in your mind during exam time or else revisit the instructions if you have any confusion. Why? So that you don't make any foolish mistake regarding instructions during examination time. So it is important.

Attempting the question paper

Read out all the questions carefully before writing anything on the answer sheet and always start with answering those questions which will carry maximum marks as well as you think are tougher or need more time to think. When you start from small questions, you will always feel the problem of answering the remaining lengthy questions. So, that's why time management is very important during exams. You can answer small questions even in the last 30 minutes but you will never be able to write enough for the lengthy questions in the end which will eventually result in less or poor marks. Manage correctly according to the questions and think like a scholar. And as always stay positive.

When you feel stuck at a question during exam

There will be a moment in your exam where you will feel stuck with some questions or a single question. You just need to be relaxed and calm, and no need to panic or be in tension. Make yourself confident and try to think or remember the answer with a cool mind. If you don't feel to give the answer at that time, make any sign or circle the question number and move on. Then try to finish it after you complete all the questions. I follow this tip a lot. Also, never ever try to think about that "stuck" question while writing the answers for other questions. This will only reduce your concentration and when you find no way out, just make a guess and attempt that question as there is no other probable way. And this may give you a chance of scoring better than before.

Answer sheet should be neat and clean

And the last tip for you is that Handwriting matters a lot for good and highest marks during your exam, as your writing makes the first impression on the examiner's mind and the examiner sometimes awards some extra marks for good handwriting. Nevertheless, handwriting affects the nature of reading. Underline the important lines to attract the examiner's attention so that he/she can make a right mindset about the answer given and also reward you with the full or maximum marks (Note: It is not always that you will get marks for your handwriting but still your writing should be good so that the examiner can read it properly).
